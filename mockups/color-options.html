SpaceX's playbook runs on feedback loops, first principles, and shipping fast enough that reality corrects you.
"The materials cost of a rocket was around 2% of the typical price. 98 cents of every dollar was going somewhere else."
The PM Translation
In product, the equivalent of "atoms" is ideas — they're essentially free. The expensive part is engineering time, coordination, and review cycles.
In Practice
Run an "idiot index" on your roadmap. For every feature, ask: what's the ratio of eng weeks to user value delivered?
