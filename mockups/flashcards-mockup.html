On Cost

Ideas are cheap.
Shipping is expensive.

It turned out that the materials cost of a rocket was around 2% of the typical price. 98 cents of every dollar was going somewhere other than what it was made of.

Tap to see the PM translation
The PM Translation

In product, the equivalent of "atoms" is ideas — they're essentially free. The expensive part is engineering time, coordination, context-switching, and review cycles. A PM who generates 100 ideas but can't ruthlessly prioritize is doing exactly what traditional aerospace did: adding cost at every layer with no accountability.

In Practice

Run an "idiot index" on your roadmap. For every feature: what's the ratio of eng weeks to actual user value? High ratio = you're adding overhead. The most over-engineered features are usually ones nobody questioned because a smart stakeholder asked for them.
